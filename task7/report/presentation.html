A comparative analysis of Content-Based vs. Collaborative Filtering

Adilet Akhmedov | Angsar Shaumen Assanali Rymgali | Bekzat Sundetkhan Sanzhar Syzdykov

Objective & Methodology

To build and evaluate recommender systems for e-commerce using the MovieLens Latest Small dataset.

  • Content-Based: TF-IDF on genres + Cosine Similarity.
  • Collaborative Filtering: SVD Matrix Factorization with mean centering.
  • Evaluation: RMSE, MAE, Precision@K, Recall@K.

Precision Analysis

Analyzing how precision degrades as K increases.

  • Analysis: Both models show high precision for top recommendations, decreasing appropriately as K increases.
  • Winner: SVD maintains slightly better consistency across K values.

Conclusion

We successfully implemented a robust recommendation pipeline. Collaborative Filtering (SVD) proved effective even on small data when properly regularized (centered), achieving an RMSE of ~0.93, comparable to the metadata-rich Content-Based approach.

  • Key Insight: Matrix Factorization captures latent tastes that simple genre matching misses.
  • Future Work: Hybrid systems could combine specific genre preferences with latent discovery.
