Introduction

Sentiment analysis automates the understanding of public opinion from vast social media streams.

  • Objective: Binary Classification (Positive/Negative) of tweets.
  • Dataset: Sentiment140.
    Sample Size: 200,000 tweets (Balanced Class Distribution)
  • Challenge: High noise (slang, sarcasm, lack of structure).

Optimized Preprocessing

Standard text cleaning often destroys sentiment signals. Our pipeline specifically addresses this.

  • Noise Removal: Cleaned URLs, @handles, and special characters.
  • Negation Handling: Crucially preserved "not", "no", "never".
  • Normalization: Lemmatization to base word forms.

Methods Overview

Conclusion

We successfully engineered a sentiment analysis pipeline achieving 78.35% accuracy, matching state-of-the-art baselines for this dataset.

  • Key Insight: Simple linear models (Logistic Regression) with TF-IDF often rival complex Deep Learning models on noisy, moderate-sized text datasets.
  • Critical Step: Negation retention was the single most important preprocessing decision.
  • Recommendation: Deep Learning (LSTM/BERT) is recommended only if the dataset scales >1M samples where context modeling becomes more beneficial than simple word co-occurrence.
